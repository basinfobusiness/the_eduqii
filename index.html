Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace (1815–1852) was an English mathematician and writer who is widely recognized as the first computer programmer.

She worked closely with Charles Babbage and translated his work on the Analytical Engine. Her notes included an algorithm designed to be processed by a machine, which is now considered the first published computer program.

Key contributions
  • Developed the first algorithm intended for a computing machine.
  • Predicted that computers could do more than calculate numbers, including composing music and creating graphics.
  • Introduced the idea of a machine that could manipulate symbols according to rules.
Legacy

Ada's vision paved the way for modern computing and inspired generations of computer scientists. Today, Ada Lovelace Day celebrates women in STEM worldwide.
